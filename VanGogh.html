Vincent Van Gogh

30. March 1853 (Netherlands) - 29. July 1890 (France)

Vincent van Gogh was a Dutch post-impressionist painter who posthumously became one of the most famous and influential figures in the history of Western art. Before deciding to be a painter in 1880, he was an art dealer, teacher, and preacher. In a decade, he created about 2,100 artworks. They include landscapes, still lifes, portraits and self-portraits, and are characterised by bold colours and dramatic, impulsive and expressive brushwork that contributed to the foundations of modern art. He was not commercially successful, and his suicide, where he shot himself in the chest, at thirty-seven came after years of depression and poverty. His brother Theo, who paid for everything he needed, inherited his collection.
